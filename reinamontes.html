Reina Montes

Leo and Edna Riley Scholarship Recipient

Dear Ms. Riley,

        I would like to express my sincere gratitude for your financial support that helped fund my college education. Notre Dame was truly an amazing experience for me and I can't imagine spending those four year anywhere else. Notre Dame is an incredible academic institution, but its strong values and religious foundation make it a place that builds character outside of the classroom and helps prepare individuals for bright futures.

        I am originally from Las Cruces, New Mexico, and attended the University of Notre Dame from 2000-2004. I lived in Cavanaugh Hall and was an American Studies major. While on campus, I was active in residence hall government, the Dome yearbook, and volunteering through Campus Girl Scouts. I also had the opportunity to spend my junior year away from campus participating in the Washington, D.C. program in the fall of 2012, where I served as an intern for then-Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton. I spent the spring of 2013 in Toledo, Spain, honing my Spanish skills and traveling through Europe.

        Following my years at Notre Dame I returned to Washington, D.C. where I served as fellow with the Congressional Hispanic Caucus Institute. I began a career in communications working for a small PR firm and then two non-profit organizations, the Heinz Family Foundation and the Girl Scouts of America. Almost 10 years ago I transitioned to the corporate sector to work for Marriott International at their corporate headquarters in Bethesda, Maryland. I still work for Marriott today and serve as Director, HR Communications, with a focus on internal communications.  

         Since graduating from Notre Dame I have remained involved with the alumni association and local clubs in the various places I have lived. While living in Washington, D.C., I served as Secretary of their club. About a year ago, I relocated to Albuquerque, New Mexico, to be closer to my family and I have gotten involved with the local club here as well. I now serve as Secretary and have organized events on behalf of the club.   


  
        Notre Dame remains a huge part of my life, as I remain close to many of the friends I made there and travel to visit them frequently. I also make an effort to return for a home football game every year and visit campus. It's such a magical place!

        Notre Dame has truly shaped who I am and influenced my core values and spiritual life. I am extremely grateful for your financial assistance that helped make this college experience and so many that followed possible for me. Thank you again for your generosity. Go Irish!

Best,
 
     
Reina Montes '04
